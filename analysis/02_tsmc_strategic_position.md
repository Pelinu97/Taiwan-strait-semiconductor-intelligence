# TSMC's Strategic Position in the Global Semiconductor Supply Chain

## Overview

Taiwan Semiconductor Manufacturing Company (TSMC) is the world's largest and most advanced contract chip manufacturer, producing semiconductors for virtually every major technology company including Apple, NVIDIA, AMD, and Qualcomm. Its strategic importance extends far beyond commerce — TSMC has become a critical node in global security architecture.

## Key Facts

- Founded in 1987 by Morris Chang in Hsinchu, Taiwan
- Produces over **90% of the world's most advanced chips** (below 7nm)
- Clients include Apple, NVIDIA, AMD, Qualcomm, Intel, and the US Department of Defense
- 2023 revenue: approximately $69.3 billion USD
- Employs over 73,000 people globally

## Why TSMC is Irreplaceable

### Technical Moat
TSMC's manufacturing processes are decades ahead of competitors. Achieving similar capability requires not just capital but an irreplaceable ecosystem of engineers, suppliers, and institutional knowledge built over 35+ years.

### Silicon Shield Theory
Taiwan's indispensability in chip manufacturing creates a deterrent against military conflict — any attack on Taiwan would trigger a global economic catastrophe, affecting adversaries as much as allies. This concept is known as the **"Silicon Shield."**

### Geographic Concentration Risk
Over 90% of advanced chip production is concentrated in a single island located 180km from mainland China — a vulnerability that has alarmed defense planners worldwide.

## US and Allied Response

- TSMC is constructing a **$40 billion fab in Arizona** (TSMC Arizona)
- Japan: TSMC opened a fab in **Kumamoto** in 2024
- Germany: TSMC announced a Dresden fab (ESMC) planned for 2027
- These moves are driven by supply chain resilience concerns, not economics

## China's Position

- China is TSMC's largest revenue market by geography
- Yet TSMC is legally prohibited from supplying advanced chips to Chinese firms under US export controls
- China's SMIC remains 2-3 generations behind TSMC in process technology
