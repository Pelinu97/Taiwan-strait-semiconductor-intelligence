# US-China Semiconductor Rivalry

## Overview

The competition between the United States and China over semiconductor technology represents one of the most consequential geopolitical rivalries of the 21st century. Semiconductors — the foundational components of modern electronics — have become a primary battleground for technological supremacy, economic dominance, and national security.

## Background

Semiconductors power everything from smartphones to fighter jets, making them a critical strategic resource. The United States has historically dominated chip design, while Taiwan — particularly through TSMC — leads in advanced manufacturing. China, despite being the world's largest consumer of semiconductors, remains heavily dependent on foreign technology.

## US Policy Actions

- **2022 CHIPS and Science Act** — $52 billion investment to rebuild domestic semiconductor manufacturing
- **Export Controls (October 2022 & 2023)** — Restricted advanced chip exports and equipment to China
- **Entity List Restrictions** — Huawei, SMIC and other Chinese firms blacklisted from accessing US technology
- **Alliance Building** — Chip 4 Alliance formed with Japan, South Korea, and Taiwan

## China's Response

- **"Made in China 2025"** initiative targeting semiconductor self-sufficiency
- Massive state subsidies through the National Integrated Circuit Industry Investment Fund ("Big Fund")
- Aggressive talent recruitment from Taiwan and South Korea
- Accelerated development of domestic EDA tools and chip equipment

## Key Tensions

- Taiwan Strait military posturing as leverage over global chip supply
- Technology decoupling accelerating between US and Chinese ecosystems
- Netherlands (ASML) pressured by US to halt EUV lithography machine exports to China
