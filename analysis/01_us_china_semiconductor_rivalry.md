US–China Semiconductor Rivalry (OSINT Assessment)

Overview

The competition between the United States and China over semiconductor technology represents one of the most consequential geopolitical rivalries of the 21st century. Semiconductors — as foundational components of modern digital and military systems — have become a central domain of technological competition, economic leverage, and national security strategy.


---

Background

Semiconductors underpin critical technologies ranging from consumer electronics to advanced weapons systems. The United States has historically maintained leadership in chip design and intellectual property, while Taiwan — particularly through TSMC — dominates advanced manufacturing. China remains the largest global consumer of semiconductors but continues to rely heavily on foreign technology inputs.


---

US Policy Actions

CHIPS and Science Act (2022)

$52 billion federal investment to strengthen domestic semiconductor manufacturing capacity


Export Controls (2022–2023)

Restrictions on advanced chip exports and semiconductor manufacturing equipment to China


Entity List Restrictions

Blacklisting of firms such as Huawei and SMIC from accessing US-origin technologies


Alliance Building Efforts

Formation of multilateral coordination frameworks such as the Chip 4 Alliance (US, Japan, South Korea, Taiwan)



---

China’s Response

Industrial Strategy

“Made in China 2025” initiative targeting semiconductor self-sufficiency


State Financing

Large-scale subsidies via the National Integrated Circuit Industry Investment Fund (“Big Fund”)


Talent Acquisition

Recruitment of semiconductor engineers from Taiwan and South Korea


Technological Development

Accelerated domestic development of EDA tools and semiconductor manufacturing equipment



---

Key Tensions

Strategic leverage of Taiwan Strait as a geopolitical pressure point

Accelerating technological decoupling between US and Chinese semiconductor ecosystems

Export restrictions on ASML EUV lithography systems under US diplomatic pressure

Fragmentation of global semiconductor supply chains into competing blocs
